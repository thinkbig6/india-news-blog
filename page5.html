India and Pakistan Quietly Agree to Ceasefire

External Affairs Minister S. Jaishankar confirmed that India and Pakistan have quietly agreed to a ceasefire, emphasizing that the United States played no role in the agreement. The ceasefire follows a period of heightened tensions between the two nations.

The agreement aims to de-escalate the situation along the Line of Control and promote peace in the region. Both sides have expressed a commitment to maintaining the ceasefire and engaging in dialogue to resolve outstanding issues.
